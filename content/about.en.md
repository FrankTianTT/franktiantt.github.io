I'm Frank Tian.

My focus: bringing AI into the physical world. That puts me at the intersection of **AI** (reinforcement learning, world models) and **robotics** (humanoid robots, key components).

### What I've done

AI by training, from the LAMDA lab of NJU. Started out in quantitative finance — my first taste of machine learning making real-world decisions. Then dove into the humanoid robot industry, building a reinforcement learning motion control system from the ground up at a major humanoid robotics company.

I also had a brief stint as a founder — [Zyuon Robotics](https://github.com/zyuonrobotics), an open-source initiative for robot hardware, software, and algorithm frameworks. The company has since dissolved and now operates as a non-profit dedicated to sharing robotics technology.

### What I write

This blog is my technical home base. I write rigorous, in-depth articles on machine learning theory, reinforcement learning, and robotics algorithms. I don't chase trends — I write about things I've actually figured out.

Scattered thoughts and industry observations live on social media.

### What I'm looking for

The goal is straightforward: build connections in the AI and robotics community, and create a place where people working on the same problems can find each other.
